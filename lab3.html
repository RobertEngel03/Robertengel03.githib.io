Single-board computers (SBCs) are compact computers with all necessary components integrated onto a single circuit board. These components typically include a processor, memory, input/output (I/O) ports, and storage interfaces. Unlike traditional desktop computers, which have separate components like a motherboard, CPU, and memory, SBCs combine these into one small, often low-cost unit. Popular examples include the Raspberry Pi, Arduino, and BeagleBone. SBCs are widely used in education, hobbyist projects, and embedded systems due to their affordability, low power consumption, and versatility. They are often utilized in applications like robotics, IoT devices, home automation, and as simple desktop replacements for basic tasks.
